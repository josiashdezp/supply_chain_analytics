# Assignment Dataset Versions (2025)

Each CSV keeps the same schema as `assignment_supply_chain_2025.csv` so the existing notebook can be reused.

Use any one of the files below as the assignment dataset to produce materially different managerial insights.

- **v01 – Urban E-commerce Surge**: Urban and electronics demand rises sharply, premium transport usage grows, and Q4
  strain creates selective service pressure.
- **v02 – Rural Last-mile Expansion**: Rural customers expand quickly, average distance grows, road dependency rises,
  and service levels become more volatile.
- **v03 – Lean Inventory Push**: Management aggressively reduces inventory and safety stock, improving cash efficiency
  at the cost of fill rate and stockout risk.
- **v04 – Premium Service Upgrade**: The network invests in faster transport and stronger provider performance,
  producing higher on-time delivery at higher cost.
- **v05 – Fuel Cost Shock**: Rising fuel prices create severe road-cost inflation and compress margins, especially on
  long-haul shipments.
- **v06 – Rail Optimization Program**: A network redesign shifts long-haul volume toward rail, reducing cost per
  kilometer while modestly extending some lead times.
- **v07 – Electronics Returns Crisis**: Electronics demand remains healthy, but a quality problem drives return rates
  sharply higher and erodes contribution margin.
- **v08 – Holiday Peak Volatility**: The year-end peak becomes much more extreme, creating forecasting misses,
  stockouts, and cost escalation in November and December.
- **v09 – Food Cold-chain Focus**: Food products get operational priority, improving freshness and service while
  increasing expedited shipments for selected lanes.
- **v10 – East Coast Supplier Disruption**: The East warehouse faces upstream disruption, causing longer lead times,
  lower fill rates, and elevated stockouts.
- **v11 – South Warehouse Turnaround**: A process-improvement program in the South warehouse improves service,
  throughput, and inventory control.
- **v12 – Cost Containment Slowdown**: Demand softens and management protects service through higher inventories,
  creating weaker turnover but lower stockout rates.
- **v13 – Sustainability Modal Shift**: The company reduces air usage and moves freight to rail, cutting average cost
  while stretching some service times.
- **v14 – Inflation and Price Reset**: Both selling prices and input costs increase materially, changing revenue and
  margin dynamics while demand cools slightly.
- **v15 – Resilient Balanced Network**: The network is rebalanced across providers and warehouses, producing strong
  service and moderate cost performance.
